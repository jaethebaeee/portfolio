# Delaware LLC Annual Report Guide

## Why File Annual Reports
Delaware LLCs must file annual reports to maintain good standing. Failure to file results in penalties and potential administrative dissolution.

## Filing Requirements

### Filing Deadline
- **Due Date:** March 1st of each year
- **Late Fee:** $300 + $50 per month late
- **Extension:** Not available - must file on time

### Filing Fee
- **Standard Fee:** $300 (paid to Delaware Secretary of State)
- **Expedited Processing:** Additional fees available
- **Electronic Filing:** Required (paper filings not accepted)

## How to File

### Online Filing (Recommended)
1. **Visit:** https://delecorp.delaware.gov/
2. **Search:** Find your company by name or file number
3. **File Report:** Use the online system
4. **Pay Fees:** Credit card or electronic payment
5. **Confirmation:** Receive email confirmation

## What Happens If You Don't File

### Penalties
- **Late Filing Fee:** $300 initial + $50/month late
- **Administrative Dissolution:** After 3 months late
- **Reinstatement Fee:** $500-$1,000
- **Legal Publication:** Required for reinstatement

### Business Impact
- **Cannot Sue:** Lose ability to file lawsuits
- **Contracts Invalid:** Some contracts become unenforceable
- **Tax Issues:** IRS may question business legitimacy
- **Banking Problems:** Banks may close accounts
- **VC Issues:** Investors require good standing

## Delaware Benefits for LLCs

### Tax Advantages
- **No State Income Tax** on LLC profits
- **Franchise Tax Only:** Minimum $400
- **Pass-through Taxation:** No double taxation

### Business-Friendly Laws
- **Strong Legal Precedent:** Court of Chancery expertise
- **Privacy Protection:** Manager/member info not public
- **Flexible Structure:** Easy to convert to corporation later
